Design a Command Center for Third-party Risk

Transforming a static dashboard to a decision surface that connects compliance, audit, evidence, and cyber posture across the ResilientX AI Platform.

The Problem

A dashboard that showed data, but didn’t drive action

ResilientX TPRM combines compliance, audits, document evidence, cyber posture, and remediation workflows. The dashboard is the entry point into that system — but as the product scaled, the dashboard didn’t evolve with it. It behaved more like a static report than a decision tool.

The consequence was significant: users were bypassing the dashboard entirely — which meant the platform’s most important entry point wasn’t doing its job.

What wasn’t working
01
Weak information hierarchy
Every metric competed for attention — nothing signaled what needed action first.
02
Difficult to scan quickly
Dense data with no visual grouping made it hard to extract meaning at a glance.
03
Didn’t reflect new capabilities
The layout predated new platform features it couldn’t surface.
04
Navigation over guidance
Users had to know where to go — the dashboard offered no next steps.

The original dashboard — data-rich but decision-poor. Every metric had equal visual weight.

Behavioral evidence

Session recordings and heatmaps showed that users typically scanned the dashboard briefly, focused on top-level KPIs, and then navigated to other modules to continue their work.

The dashboard was visible — but not functioning as a decision surface.

Research & Discovery

Why users were skipping the TPRM dashboard

The behavioral data confirmed there was a problem. But to redesign with confidence, I needed to understand the cause — not just the symptom. I combined session analysis, click maps, stakeholder interviews, and competitor research to build a clear picture of what users actually needed.

01
Users bypassed the dashboard entirely
Session recordings showed users going straight to the Companies table — the dashboard was not part of their workflow.
02
Goal-oriented users execute — they don't explore
Click maps confirmed widgets had near-zero engagement. Users arrived with a task. The dashboard needed to route them, not inform them.
03
Best-in-class dashboards are launchers, not posters
Across Panorays, OneTrust and SecurityScorecard — every widget led somewhere actionable. Data without a path forward is noise.

"The dashboard was visible — but it wasn't part of anyone's decision process. The redesign needed to change that."

Challenge & Approach

One dashboard. Three users. One hierarchy decision.

Through stakeholder conversations with the CEO and CTO — combined with sales feedback on what customers valued most — I identified the core design challenge: one dashboard had to serve three completely different users, each arriving with different questions, different urgency, and different definitions of what mattered.

To decide what goes where, I used three inputs: user urgency (how much time each user actually has), business value (what sales and leadership told us customers care about most), and competitor analysis across Panorays, OneTrust, SecurityScorecard and UpGuard to understand what the best TPRM dashboards prioritize.

The Solution

Turning TPRM complexity into one clear control layer

Designed as a control layer, not a workflow replacement — the dashboard surfaces key signals across compliance, audits, documents, cyber posture, and tasks, and routes users to the right place for detailed work.

Outcome

From static data to signals that drive action

After launch, Hotjar data confirmed the dashboard works as a control layer — users engage first with top-level signals and move more predictably into deeper analysis when sections contain meaningful data.

What we observed
01
Faster orientation
Users focus first on top-level KPIs to understand overall program posture.
02
Clearer exploration
Clicks cluster around key signals and intended entry points.
03
Progressive exploration (data-dependent)
When data is available, users move from summary signals into deeper content and module links.
System insight
●
Engagement concentrates where signal strength is highest. Sections dependent on supplier input show lower interaction when data is unavailable — highlighting the importance of clear empty and in-progress states.
