Onboarding as a System

Designing a Scalable Supplier Intake for Enterprise TPRM

Transforming a manual onboarding bottleneck into a scalable, data-controlled system — reducing time-to-value and enabling enterprise teams to onboard 50–500 suppliers efficiently.

The Problem

Onboarding built for one supplier at a time, in a system that needed to handle hundreds

Supplier onboarding is the entry point to all TPRM workflows — compliance, audits, and risk monitoring. Yet the experience was designed around manual, one-by-one data entry.

For enterprise customers managing 50–500 suppliers, this didn't scale — turning onboarding into a bottleneck rather than a starting point.

What wasn't working
01
Manual entry at scale
Session recordings showed users consistently hesitating on field requirements, scrolling repeatedly, and spending significantly more time than the task warranted — even for a single supplier.
02
No bulk operations
Teams managing large vendor portfolios had no way to import existing data, forcing them into repetitive manual work. The workaround was offline spreadsheets and backend engineering imports.
03
Unclear field requirements
No distinction between required and optional fields — users hesitated, skipped fields, and had to go back and complete records.
04
Downstream data risk
Incorrect data entered during onboarding propagated silently into compliance and audit workflows, reducing trust in the system.

The original form — one-by-one entry, no field hierarchy, no bulk support.

Impact

As a result, teams bypassed the product entirely — relying on spreadsheets and backend workarounds — delaying time-to-value and weakening the platform's role in enterprise onboarding.

Onboarding wasn't failing because users couldn't fill a form. It was failing because the product wasn't designed for how enterprise teams actually work.

Research & Discovery

Why users abandoned the onboarding flow entirely

Session analysis confirmed the workarounds. But to design the right solution I needed to understand why users chose spreadsheets over the product — and what mental model they already had for bulk data work.

01 — Key Insight
Users already lived in spreadsheets
Source: Clarity session recordings · user interviews
Design Decision
Meet users where they already work — accept CSV
The solution had to start from their existing workflow, not force a new one. CSV import removed the copy-paste entirely.
02 — Key Insight
Late validation caused the most frustration
Source: Support tickets · user interviews
Design Decision
Inline validation had to be a core architecture, not a layer
Errors needed to surface per-row, per-field, before import — not after a full submission attempt.
03 — Key Insight
Best-in-class bulk imports use guided mapping
Source: Salesforce · Intercom · HubSpot · Airtable
Design Decision
Progressive disclosure was the right mental model
A three-step flow — upload, map, review — shows users only what they need at each moment and builds confidence before committing.
Challenge & Approach

One import flow. Three failure modes to prevent.

The core design challenge wasn't building a file upload — it was building trust. Operations teams are responsible for data accuracy. Any flow that risked corrupting supplier records would be abandoned immediately.

I mapped the three failure modes users feared most — wrong field mapping, silent errors, and partial imports — and designed the flow so each step directly prevented one. The approach was shaped by user urgency (speed matters, but not at the cost of accuracy), stakeholder input on what caused the most downstream damage, and competitor analysis of how Salesforce, HubSpot and Airtable handle bulk imports.

The Solution

A guided import flow that meets users where they already work

Upload a CSV, map columns to system fields, review validated records before committing — each step designed to prevent a specific failure mode and build confidence throughout.

01
Three-step progressive flow
Upload, map, review — each step surfaces only what's needed at that moment, reducing cognitive load and preventing errors before they happen.
02
Intelligent column mapping
Header matching auto-suggests field mappings with sample data previews — most well-structured CSVs are ready to import in a few clicks.
03
Inline per-row validation
Errors surface per-field before submission — with clear remediation paths — so bad data never reaches the system.
04
Saved mapping templates
Teams with recurring imports can save column configurations and reuse them — turning a 3-minute task into a 30-second one.

Outcome

Operations teams stopped worrying about onboarding

The redesigned flow eliminated the workarounds entirely. Teams now onboard hundreds of suppliers in a single session — without spreadsheet exports, backend engineering, or data correction tickets. With 30 days of data the signals are clear; return rate and session duration would confirm adoption at scale.
