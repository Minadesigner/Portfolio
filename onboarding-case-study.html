The Problem

The old flow was slow, error-prone, and manual

[PLACEHOLDER] Supplier onboarding required operations teams to manually enter each supplier's details through a multi-step form with over 15 required fields. Each entry took approximately 3 minutes, and with hundreds of suppliers to onboard per quarter, the process consumed entire workdays.

[PLACEHOLDER] The form lacked validation until final submission, meaning data errors were only caught after the full entry was complete. Teams reported a 12% error rate on submitted records, leading to downstream issues in compliance workflows and supplier communication.

[PLACEHOLDER] Bulk operations were unsupported. Teams resorted to filling spreadsheets offline and having engineers import them via backend scripts — a fragile, unscalable workaround that introduced its own class of formatting and deduplication errors.

Behavioral evidence
  • [PLACEHOLDER] Session recordings showed users switching between the onboarding form and external spreadsheets to copy-paste data field by field
  • [PLACEHOLDER] Support tickets related to onboarding errors accounted for 23% of total platform support volume
  • [PLACEHOLDER] Operations managers reported spending 4+ hours per week correcting supplier data entered through the manual flow

The Solution

Bulk import with guided field mapping

[PLACEHOLDER] The redesigned flow replaced manual entry entirely with a three-step process: upload a CSV file, map columns to system fields through a guided interface, and review validated records before import. Each step provides progressive disclosure — showing only what the user needs at that moment.

[PLACEHOLDER] The field mapping step uses intelligent defaults based on column header matching, reducing configuration to a few clicks for well-structured files. An inline validation layer highlights errors per-row and per-field before submission, eliminating the delayed feedback loop of the old system.

[PLACEHOLDER] For teams with non-standard CSV formats, the mapper provides visual previews of sample data alongside target fields, allowing users to verify mappings without leaving the flow. Saved mapping templates let teams reuse configurations across recurring imports.

Design Process

How the solution took shape

[PLACEHOLDER] The design process balanced speed of delivery with thoroughness of research. Starting from observed user pain points, I moved through rapid prototyping to validate core interaction patterns before committing to high-fidelity implementation.

User research & pain mapping

[PLACEHOLDER] Interviewed 6 operations team members and observed onboarding sessions. Mapped the end-to-end flow to identify friction, error-prone steps, and workaround patterns.

Progressive disclosure framework

[PLACEHOLDER] Designed the three-step flow to reduce cognitive load at each stage. Each step only surfaces information relevant to the current decision, preventing overwhelm.

Validation-first architecture

[PLACEHOLDER] Built inline validation as a core design principle rather than an afterthought. Errors surface immediately with clear remediation paths, preventing bad data from entering the system.
